How to Know if You Have COVID-19

Identifying the coronavirus from other sicknesses is often difficult. The coronavirus can enter the body via respiratory droplets. Coughs, sneezes, and the rubbing of eyes and nose are common passageways that the virus could enter. Even though you cannot feel that the virus is in your body, there are symptoms that people have in which the coronavirus gives. The coronavirus is like that of the common cold. You will experience stuffy nose, sneezing, coughing and sore throat. The common cold symptoms will advance to symptoms of the flu. In severe cases, people will feel shortness of breath. These are the common ways to identify the coronavirus.

How to Protect Yourself

According to the CDC, there is no common cure for the COVID-19. The only way to protect yourself is avoid exposure. If you are in a public facility, it is advised to wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water. Especially after sneezing, coughing, or blowing your nose. If soap and water are unavailable, use hand sanitizer with 60% alcohol. Another main tip to protecting yourself against the COVID-19 is avoiding others that are sick. Stay home as much as possible.

How to Protect Others

Because the coronavirus is hard to identify, staying home if you are sick is an extreme factor in protecting others. YouTuber ViolinMD stresses that wearing a face mask and covering your mouth when you sneeze or cough is also a key necessity for protecting others even if you do not have the virus.
